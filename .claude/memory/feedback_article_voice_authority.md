name: Article voice — own authority, never curator
description: Never position ROBOHOGAR as reading/curating other reviews — speak with own authority

---

Never reference that we "read", "analyzed", or "compiled" other reviews/analyses in article copy. Phrases like "hemos leído todas las reviews", "tras leer 10+ análisis", "cruzando datos de reviews" position ROBOHOGAR as a curator of others' work — the reader will think "better go to the original source".

**Why:** The reader must feel ROBOHOGAR IS the source. Citing a specific reviewer (e.g., "WIRED lo probó durante un mes") as evidence is fine — that's journalism. But framing the entire article as "we read what others said" destroys authority and gives the reader a reason to leave.

**How to apply:** In `/content-draft`, never open with or lean on "we reviewed the reviews." State findings directly as our own analysis. Use specific source citations inline as supporting evidence, not as the article's reason to exist.
